Domestic incinerators are a useful to combust household waste, medical waste, slaughter waste, etc. instead of discharging it in a landfill. Furthermore, heat and energy may be recovered and it helps to avoid open burning of municipal waste which creates much more harmful emissions and endanger human health and environment. Costs for these incinerators range from very high to low, but it is often possible to construct the incinerators by instructed community members and with local materials such as bricks and steel. The waste should be separated previously in order to retain organics or recyclable material before incineration. The ashes can contain toxic substances and heavy metals and should therefore, if possible, be disposed of safely.
